Swansea Heads House

斯旺西海角之家  ·  Anthrosite  ·  Swansea, NSW, Australia
David Boyle Award – Residential Architecture Houses (New)
Design Reading
Swansea Heads House by Anthrosite occupies a narrow coastal headland at Swansea, New South Wales, where the discipline of building within a marine vegetation and coastal buffer zone concentrated every design decision. The program resolves into two pavilions—one opening toward the Pacific horizon, one turned inward—organised around a protected courtyard that channels prevailing north-easterly breezes while shielding residents from unrelenting coastal gales. Horizontal datum lines threaded through the facade and internal screens echo the layered sandstone stratigraphy exposed along Crabbs Beach, binding architecture to geology without literal mimicry. The palette of board-formed concrete, spotted gum hardwood, and marine-grade steel was chosen for resilience against salt corrosion, yet each element reads as deliberately composed rather than merely functional. At the 2026 AIA NSW Regional Architecture Awards, the project received three accolades: the David Boyle Award, the Named Award for Residential Architecture – Houses (New), and the Named Award for Interior Architecture—a sweep confirming Anthrosite's mastery of coastal residential design as simultaneously technical performance and spatial craft.
客戶版本
此宅矗立於新南威爾斯州斯旺西岬角，兩座翼樓圍合出面朝海洋、背風庇護的庭院。立面水平線條呼應海灘砂岩的地層紋理；原漿混凝土、硬木與船用鋼材在鹽霧環境中經久耐用。建築充分導入東北向海風自然通風，幾乎免除機械冷氣。2026 年 AIA NSW 地區建築獎三項殊榮——大衛·博伊爾獎、住宅（新建）命名獎及室內建築命名獎——印證 Anthrosite 在技術性能與空間工藝上的高超整合。
Victorian Awards 2026

House in a Garden

花園中的家  ·  Edition Office  ·  Kew, Melbourne, Australia
Harold Desbrowe Annear Award – Residential Architecture Houses (New)
Design Reading
House in a Garden by Edition Office (Aaron Roberts and Jono Brener) sits within a prestigious residential precinct in Kew, Melbourne, on a low-lying site within the Birrarung and Yarra floodplain where the firm chose to interpret a difficult constraint as a generative invitation. Rather than elevating the house on a conventional plinth, Edition Office lifted the primary living volume into the tree canopy, suspending two pavilion-like wings on slender columns above a communal garden that flows uninterrupted beneath the structure. A timber batten screen wraps the elevated platform, filtering light into the interior while casting shifting shadow patterns across the garden below. The collaboration with landscape firm Eckersley Garden Architecture is inseparable from the architectural resolution: the garden inhabits the space beneath the house, making the canopy rather than the ground the primary domestic datum. The project received the Harold Desbrowe Annear Award—the Victorian Architecture Awards' highest residential honour—at the 2026 ceremony. Photography by Maxime Delvaux.
客戶版本
此宅位於墨爾本 Kew 的 Birrarung 沖積平原，Edition Office 選擇將主要生活空間抬升至樹冠層，讓完整的庭院在建築下方自由延展。木格柵圍護的高架平台以光影篩選向內的陽光；兩座翼樓分別容納起居與臥寢機能，以玻璃廊橋相連。與 Eckersley Garden Architecture 的景觀合作使樹冠成為主要的生活基準面。作品榮獲 2026 年維多利亞州建築獎哈羅德·德斯布羅·安尼爾獎，是住宅（新建）類別最高肯定。
Victorian Awards 2026

Hislop Hill House

希斯洛普山丘之家  ·  Clare Cousins Architects  ·  Melbourne, Victoria, Australia
Architecture Award – Residential Architecture Houses (New)
Design Reading
Hislop Hill House by Clare Cousins Architects occupies a mid-century suburban block in Melbourne's inner ring, designed for a family returning from an extended period in Hong Kong who wanted a deeply local architectural response. Clare Cousins resolved a steeply sloping site through four distinct split-level platforms that step with the natural fall of the land, creating a vertical sequence of connected but subtly differentiated living territories. A fenceless native garden wraps the perimeter, intentionally blurring the boundary between private garden and public streetscape. The facade combines face brick—sympathetic to the neighbouring postwar suburban palette—with spotted gum timber battens that weather naturally. Two mezzanine levels create double-height spaces that draw northern sunlight deep into the plan. The material honesty throughout—exposed concrete, untreated timber, handmade ceramic tiles—requires no disguise because each material is placed where it performs best. Hislop Hill House received an Architecture Award for Residential Architecture – Houses (New) at the 2026 Victorian Architecture Awards. Photography by Tom Ross.
客戶版本
此宅由 Clare Cousins Architects 設計，位於墨爾本內環住宅區，為一對由香港回流的家庭量身打造。四個不同高度的平台順應地勢坡降層疊展開，形成具有差異性的生活序列。無圍欄的原生植栽庭院模糊了私密與公共邊界；面磚與硬木格柵的組合承接戰後郊區建築語彙。兩層夾層空間將北面陽光引入室內深處。此案榮獲 2026 年維多利亞州建築獎住宅（新建）類建築獎。
Dezeen 2026

Tokyo Ballet House

東京芭蕾之家  ·  Kenta Sano & Associates  ·  Toshima, Tokyo, Japan
Dezeen Featured Project – July 2026
Design Reading
Tokyo Ballet House by Kenta Sano & Associates occupies a corner site in Toshima, Tokyo, where the architects were commissioned to simultaneously build a professional dance studio and a private family residence, stacking two entirely different programmes into a single urban vertical. The ground and second floors are given over to a full-height glazed dance studio, its transparent facade directly engaging the street and transforming each rehearsal into a kind of sustained public performance visible to passersby. Above, the third and fourth floors pivot to become a private home, separated from the studio's acoustic demands through a thick structural floor plate and carefully engineered sound isolation. The residence shares the same material discipline of raw concrete structure and large-format glazing but deploys recessed terraces and strategic openings to recover the degree of privacy impossible at street level. Dezeen featured the project in July 2026 as a landmark example of Tokyo's increasingly hybrid residential typologies that merge cultural programmes with domestic life.
客戶版本
此作位於東京豊島區，由佐野健太建築設計事務所設計，將舞蹈教室與私人住宅疊合為一棟垂直城市建築。低樓層的全高玻璃舞蹈工作室讓街道行人得以目睹每一次排練，公共表演與私人藝術修習的界線被刻意模糊。上方樓層轉化為家宅空間，以厚實樓板進行聲學隔離，並藉退縮陽台重新取得私密性。此作品於 2026 年 7 月獲 Dezeen 以「城市混合建築新典範」為題專題報導。
A' Design Award 2025

Valencia House

瓦倫西亞別墅  ·  Padovani Arquitetos  ·  São Paulo countryside, Brazil
A' Design Award Platinum – Architecture  ·  iF Design Award 2024
Design Reading
Valencia House by Padovani Arquitetos, led by Lucas Padovani, rises within the verdant countryside outside São Paulo, Brazil, as a 1,500-square-metre summer retreat accommodating seven suites, an indoor lap pool, gymnasium, and generous entertaining spaces without competing visually with the surrounding landscape. The parti is resolutely horizontal—a series of low planes and overhanging slabs that echo the layered horizon of the agricultural land beyond—and this emphatic groundedness becomes the primary architectural gesture. Structural walls of off-form concrete anchor the building to the site while interiors are finished in natural stone, untreated timber, and handwoven textiles sourced from the São Paulo hinterland. The indoor lap pool occupies a dedicated glazed pavilion set slightly apart from the main volume and connected by a covered walkway. Floor-to-ceiling sliding glass panels dissolve the boundary between the living rooms and the garden. Valencia House won the Platinum award in the A' Design Award's Architecture category for 2025 and the iF Design Award 2024, establishing Padovani Arquitetos as one of South America's most recognised contemporary residential practices.
客戶版本
此別墅由 Padovani Arquitetos 操刀，坐落於聖保羅近郊，佔地 1,500 平方公尺，設有七間套房、室內游泳池與健身房。極致水平的建築輪廓呼應周邊農業地景的層疊地平線，讓建築與自然和諧共存。現拌混凝土結構牆、天然石材與未加工木材構成室內涼爽而不失溫度的氛圍。玻璃泳池館以廊道與主樓相連，形成獨立的感官環境。此作榮獲 2025 年 A' 設計獎建築類白金獎及 2024 年 iF 設計獎。
Victorian Awards 2026

Bruce Street

布魯斯街之家  ·  MUIR Architecture  ·  Melbourne, Victoria, Australia
Architecture Award – Residential Architecture Houses (New)
Design Reading
Bruce Street by MUIR Architecture (led by Amy Muir) navigates a sloping suburban block in Melbourne's eastern suburbs where the ground falls away from the street, enabling the house to begin as a single storey at the front and expand to two storeys at the rear. Rather than treating this sectional shift as a pragmatic consequence to be suppressed, MUIR made it the generative idea: the rear elevation reads as a symmetrical sectional icon—a chimney-like central clerestory flanked by gently raking rooflines—while the front facade carves back from this extruded profile to create a generous, unfenced forecourt where the garden folds into the street without barrier. Three distinct fronts are organised around a layered landscape strategy that affords privacy without enclosure, enabling chance conversation between residents and passing neighbours increasingly rare in Melbourne's suburbs. Within, a double-height corridor aligns solar panels flush with the parapet while directing high southern light into the centre of the plan. Walls are lined with warm veneer panelling and brickwork at human height, while white-sculpted ceilings negotiate structure and roofline above. Bruce Street received an Architecture Award for Residential Architecture – Houses (New) at the 2026 Victorian Architecture Awards.
客戶版本
此作由 MUIR Architecture 的 Amy Muir 主持，位於墨爾本東郊一塊由前向後傾斜的地塊。建築沿坡勢從前方單層延伸至後方兩層，後立面呈現具標誌性的對稱剖面——中央天窗搭配向兩側和緩傾斜的屋面。無圍欄的前院讓庭院與街道連成一體，鼓勵居民與鄰居的偶遇交流。雙層挑空走廊引入高處南面天光，木飾板與磚工構成溫暖耐用的室內表面。此作榮獲 2026 年維多利亞州建築獎住宅（新建）類建築獎。

Palmerston Street House

帕默斯頓街之家  ·  Robert Simeoni Architects  ·  Carlton, Melbourne, Australia
John & Phyllis Murphy Award – Residential Architecture Houses (Alterations and Additions)
Design Reading
Palmerston Street House by Robert Simeoni Architects stands in Carlton, an inner Melbourne suburb where successive waves of European migration left visible traces in layers of 1950s and 1960s alterations laid over Victorian-era cottages. The brief was to renew the building's interior spaces while leaving the house largely untouched from the street, preserving the suburban contribution of an earlier era of migrant making. Simeoni's strategy turns on a series of simple but precisely calibrated spatial manoeuvres: the oversized garage becomes a light-filled meeting room and transitional threshold space opening simultaneously to the street and to the rear garden; an upstairs bedroom is selectively removed to reveal a concealed outdoor terrace that sits invisibly above the roofline. Throughout, the material language deliberately echoes the modest ingenuity of the original migrant renovations—off-the-shelf tiles in unexpected configurations, painted render in unexpected colours, ironmongery chosen for directness rather than luxury. Palmerston Street House received the John and Phyllis Murphy Award for Residential Architecture – Houses (Alterations and Additions) at the 2026 Victorian Architecture Awards.
客戶版本
帕默斯頓街之家由 Robert Simeoni Architects 設計，位於墨爾本卡爾頓，保留了 1950 至 60 年代歐洲移民對維多利亞時期排屋所做改建的印跡。設計策略以簡潔的空間操作為核心：車庫改造為兼具街道與花園連接的光線充盈會議空間；拆去一間臥室後，屋頂線上浮現一處隱藏露台。所有材料選用市售規格品以意想不到的組合方式配置，呼應早年移民改建的樸素工藝精神。此案榮獲 2026 年維多利亞州建築獎改建增建類最高榮譽——約翰與菲莉斯·墨菲獎。
ArchDaily Brasil 2026

Casa das 7 Árvores

七棵樹之家  ·  Hersen Mendes Arquitetura  ·  Brasília, DF, Brazil
ArchDaily Brasil Obra do Ano 2026 – Finalist (Residential)
Design Reading
Casa das 7 Árvores by Hersen Mendes Arquitetura (Anastácia Hersen and Matheus Mendes) occupies a gently sloping site within the Brazilian cerrado biome in Brasília, where the firm confronted a compelling paradox: build a 512-square-metre family home without disturbing the seven mature trees the owners had spent years cultivating. The architectural response resolves through a choreography of volumetric negotiation: sections of the house ground themselves on the site's flattest terrain while other sections are suspended above root zones on slender piloti, allowing the cerrado's distinctive vegetation to inhabit the interstitial spaces beneath. This alternation between grounded and hovering volumes creates a dynamic sectional rhythm that reads simultaneously as rooted in and levitated above the land. The primary wall material is ecological brick—unfired and compressed, carrying significantly lower embodied carbon than conventional kiln-fired masonry—while Bubbledeck flat concrete slabs span the longer structural intervals with minimal depth. A photovoltaic array integrated within the roof plane generates the majority of the home's annual energy demand. Photography by Joana França. The project placed third in the residential category of the ArchDaily Brasil 2026 Obra do Ano Award.
客戶版本
七棵樹之家由 Hersen Mendes Arquitetura 設計，座落於巴西利亞熱帶莽原（cerrado）生物群落，面積 512 平方公尺。設計核心課題是在保護地主悉心培育多年的七棵大樹前提下建造住宅。建築師以交替的落地與架空量體回應——平坦地段直接落地，根系分布處以細柱將建築懸浮其上，讓莽原植被在建築底部自由穿越。未燒製生態磚與 Bubbledeck 混凝土樓板構成主要結構，配合屋頂光伏板滿足大部分能源需求。此案獲選 ArchDaily Brasil 2026 年度建築獎決賽入圍，居住宅類第三名。
